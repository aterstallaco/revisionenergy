The Opportunity: From Waste to Value

Landfill gas is a natural byproduct of decomposition. Instead of letting this potent greenhouse gas escape, LFG-to-RNG projects capture it, turning an environmental liability into a valuable, low-carbon fuel. This section explores the lifecycle of gas production and the fundamental process.

The Risks: A Nuanced Perspective

A smart investment requires a clear-eyed view of the challenges. These range from policy uncertainty and market volatility to public perception and the competitive landscape of different RNG feedstocks. This section breaks down the key risks investors must navigate.

Carbon Intensity by Feedstock

Under Low Carbon Fuel Standards (LCFS), the value of credits is tied to a fuel's Carbon Intensity (CI) score. Lower (or negative) scores are more valuable. This chart shows why dairy manure is often prioritized by investors over landfill gas.

Policy Uncertainty

Future of federal (RFS) and state (LCFS) programs is subject to political change, creating risk for the primary revenue streams.

Market Volatility

LCFS credit markets have seen prices fall due to oversupply, a "victim of its own success" scenario that can deter new investment.

"Greenwashing" Critiques

Public and regulatory pressure can threaten the "social license to operate" and influence the policy frameworks that support RNG.

The Verdict & Strategic Recommendations

Landfills are a smart investment for RNG, but only for strategic investors prepared to navigate risks distinct from conventional energy markets. Success requires proactive management of policy, technology, and public perception.

1. Deep Due Diligence

Analyze the regulatory landscape and model financials with credit price volatility scenarios.

2. Prioritize Robust Tech

Select flexible upgrading systems that can handle the landfill's entire gas lifecycle, especially low-quality gas.

3. Secure Offtake Agreements

Mitigate market risk with long-term contracts for RNG and its environmental credits.

4. Manage Stakeholders

Engage with communities and proactively communicate environmental benefits to build a "social license to operate."
